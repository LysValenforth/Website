My Toxic Love Triangle with the Internet

Feb 6, 2026

Introduction

I confess and admit to the fact that I am guilty of cheating on my oldest, most trusted friend. And at this moment it feels as though I've committed a serious offense. Right now (at 11:32 PM), there is complete silence everywhere but the steady hum of the electric fan I'm sitting beside. I've had two cups of coffee so far tonight; I used that 3 in 1 Mix coffee because I needed something to keep my eyes from closing completely. On the other side of me is an empty yellow pad of paper; I can see it looking at me as if to say... “write your assignment for tomorrow.”

The scene of the crime.

The Body

In order to realize why this experience is so wrong for me, you need to look at where I came from. The day I was tall enough to get to a keyboard in a store (the first time) was when I started using Google as an extension of myself. That is to say we grew up together. For years, Google was the way I accessed Y8.com (games), a method to figure out the Redstone tutorials in Minecraft.

Although the honeymoon phase will eventually come to an end, I noticed the first signs of distress within my new relationship while working on a significant research project during SHS specifically Grade 12. During this time, I requested a quote from my AI "friend" along with a reference (source) to support my argument.

Conclusion

Now where do I stand with all of this? I'm in a toxic relationship between two forms of technology - a digital love triangle. I may be considered a cheater for using them both at the same time. However, being real here, AI is going to be around forever.
